WHY POWERHOUSE

Powerhouse sits at the intersection of arts, science, design, and technology – and we're undergoing a once-in-a-generation $1.2 billion transformation across four Sydney locations: Ultimo, Parramatta, Castle Hill, and Sydney Observatory. As a volunteer, you'll have front-row access to this evolution, working alongside curators, educators, and designers who are reshaping what museums can be.

Whether you're guiding visitors through groundbreaking exhibitions, handling objects from our 500,000+ collection, or supporting major cultural events, you'll gain invaluable skills, expand your network, and join a community of 500+ passionate volunteers. Be part of Sydney's cultural future. Volunteer at Powerhouse.

FAQ

DO I NEED EXPERIENCE TO VOLUNTEER AT POWERHOUSE?

No experience necessary! We welcome volunteers from all backgrounds and walks of life. Whether you're a student, professional, retiree, or career-changer, what matters most is your enthusiasm and willingness to learn. We provide comprehensive training for every role, and many of our most valued volunteers had no museum experience when they started. Your unique perspective and life experience are what make our volunteer community so rich and diverse.

WHAT TIME COMMITMENT IS REQUIRED?

Most volunteer roles require a minimum commitment of 3-4 hours per week or one shift per fortnight, though this varies by role. We understand life gets busy, so we offer flexible scheduling to work around your study, work, or family commitments. Some roles like event support are more casual and operate on an on-call basis for special exhibitions and programs. During your application, you'll discuss your availability and we'll find a role that fits your schedule.

WHAT ROLES ARE CURRENTLY AVAILABLE?

We have opportunities across multiple areas including Gallery Guides, Event Support, Collections Assistants, Conservation Helpers, Youth Program Facilitators, Digital Archives Volunteers, Marketing & Outreach support, and Administrative roles. Each position offers different experiences – from engaging directly with visitors to working behind the scenes with our world-class collection. Visit the 'Volunteer Opportunities' section above to explore specific roles, or attend one of our monthly information sessions to learn more about what's currently available.

HOW DO I APPLY TO BECOME A VOLUNTEER?

Getting started is easy! Click the 'Register Now' button to complete our online expression of interest form (takes about 5 minutes). Once submitted, our Volunteer Coordinator will contact you within 2 weeks to invite you to our next volunteer information session. These sessions run monthly both in-person and online, where you'll learn more about available roles, meet current volunteers, and ask questions. After attending, you'll complete a formal application, provide two references, and undergo a Working With Children Check (WWCC) if required for your role. The entire process typically takes 4-6 weeks from expression of interest to your first shift.

WHAT TRAINING AND SUPPORT WILL I RECEIVE?

Every volunteer receives comprehensive role-specific training before their first shift. This includes an orientation to Powerhouse's history and mission, workplace health and safety training, customer service skills (for public-facing roles), and hands-on training with experienced volunteers. Gallery Guides receive specialized training on current exhibitions and collection highlights. You'll also have access to ongoing professional development workshops, quarterly briefings on new exhibitions, and continuous support from our Volunteer Coordinator and staff mentors. We invest in your growth because your success is our success.

WHAT ARE THE BENEFITS OF VOLUNTEERING AT POWERHOUSE?

Beyond the joy of contributing to Sydney's cultural life, volunteers receive free access to all Powerhouse exhibitions and programs, invitations to exclusive volunteer previews and behind-the-scenes tours, professional development workshops, networking opportunities with museum professionals and fellow volunteers, and certificates of service for your resume. You'll gain hands-on experience in the museum sector, develop new skills, and become part of a vibrant community of 500+ volunteers. Many volunteers tell us the friendships they've made and the unique experiences they've had are the greatest rewards of all.

CAN INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS OR VISA HOLDERS VOLUNTEER?

Yes! International students and visa holders are welcome to volunteer at Powerhouse. Volunteering is considered unpaid work and generally doesn't affect your visa conditions, though we recommend checking with your visa provider if you have specific concerns. International students find volunteering an excellent way to build Australian work experience, improve English language skills, expand their professional network, and connect with Sydney's cultural community. We value the diverse perspectives our international volunteers bring to our team.

WHICH POWERHOUSE LOCATIONS CAN I VOLUNTEER AT?

Volunteer opportunities are currently available at Sydney Observatory (The Rocks, on Gadigal land) and Powerhouse Castle Hill (on Dharug land). With Powerhouse Parramatta opening in late 2026 and Powerhouse Ultimo undergoing revitalization, exciting new volunteer opportunities will be announced as these projects progress. During your application, you can indicate your location preferences, and we'll match you with roles that suit your travel accessibility. Some administrative and digital roles can even be done remotely.

AM I COVERED BY INSURANCE WHILE VOLUNTEERING?

Yes, absolutely. All Powerhouse volunteers are covered by comprehensive public liability and personal accident insurance during rostered volunteer shifts, training sessions, and official museum events. Your safety and wellbeing are our top priority. All volunteers also receive workplace health and safety induction training, and we maintain strict protocols to ensure a safe environment for everyone. If you have any concerns about insurance or safety at any time, our Volunteer Coordinator is always available to discuss them.
